The one idea

General seat + folder-pointing

Everyone uses the same setup. Behaviour comes from the folder you open, not your job title. Open the folder for your task → Claude reads that folder's brain (CLAUDE.md) → it already knows how to help.

The Drive = the brain

The 5. Claude OS folder on our Team Drive holds the voice, the avatar, the offers, and a brain for every kind of work. This is what makes everything on-brand.

Connectors = the hands

Trello, Slack, Google Workspace, Canva (and Go High Level) let Claude actually do the work — pull your tasks, send the email, build the design — not just chat about it.

Why this matters: most of our team wears several hats. A person who is Admin + HR + Programs doesn't need a special install — their role card just points at several folders, and the folder they open steers Claude. Multi-hat needs zero special setup.

The 3 golden rules
1
The Team Drive (5. Claude OS) is the single source of truth — always.
2
Nothing is live until Jeannie approves it. Drafts stay in Drafts.
3
When something changes, update the file — not just our memory.
The review gate is how the system learns

Jeannie isn't just QA. When a piece is excellent, it's promoted into that folder's samples/gold with a note on why. When something misses, the reason goes into samples/avoid. Over time every folder gets smarter on its own cycle.

For each team member

Set up Claude Cowork in 5 steps

First time setting up your TWA project in Claude Cowork? Do these five things — about 10 minutes.

  1. Name the project The Wide Awakening (same for everyone).
  2. Paste the Project Instructions from _AI Operations/MASTER - Claude Project Instructions.md, then add one line: "My role is [your seat]. My home folders + prompts are in _AI Operations/Roles/[my card].md."
  3. Connect the folder G:\…\5. Claude OS (connect the 5. Claude OS folder specifically; drive letter is usually G: via Google Drive for Desktop). This is the engine.
  4. Connect your tools — everyone: Trello · Slack · Google Workspace · Canva, plus your role-specific ones (Go High Level; Gmail/Calendar/booking). This is what lets Claude do, not just draft.
  5. Turn Memory ON so Claude remembers your role and recurring work between sessions.

Then open your role card and try a starter prompt — or grab one from the Prompt Playbook tab above. First move: "Pull my Trello tasks and let's knock out the first one."
